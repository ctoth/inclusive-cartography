## Key Citations for Follow-up

- **Fleming, L. (1990) "Tactual mapping"** — The baseline review Perkins extends. Any attempt to frame the history of tactile cartography must start here; Perkins positions himself explicitly as the post-Fleming progress report.

- **Gardiner, A. (2001) PhD thesis** — Cited repeatedly as the most comprehensive treatment of production technology and countryside tactile maps. It supplies the four-family production taxonomy and the thermoform vs. microcapsule comparison that anchors Section IV. Access path: "available from author" — may now be in Manchester's institutional repository.

- **Gleeson, B.J. (1999) "Can technology overcome the disabling city?"** — Supplies the critical-disability / medical-model critique that Perkins uses to reframe the entire field in Section VI. Essential for any project applying a critical lens to inclusive-cartography protocol design.

- **Golledge, R.G. ed. (1999) "Wayfinding behavior..."** — The dominant cognitive/wayfinding reference for the field; Perkins treats it as the authoritative synthesis of the cognitive strand.

- **Perkins, C. and Gardiner, A. (1997) "'What I really, really want...'"** — Participatory-design paper by the present author that grounds Section VI's recommendation for user involvement; a methodological model for inclusive-cartography protocol design.
