## Key Citations for Follow-up

- **[24] Taylor, Dey, Siewiorek, Smailagic (2016). Customizable 3D Printed Tactile Maps as Interactive Overlays.** ASSETS '16, DOI 10.1145/2982142.2982167. The directly competing 3D-printed tactile map paper at the same venue — critical for adjudicating technique priority and for the inclusive-cartography review's technologies-and-tools question. Already in the collection as Taylor_2016_Customizable3DPrintedTactile.
- **[30] Götzelmann & Schneider (2016). CapCodes: Capacitive 3D Printable Identification and On-screen Tracking for Tangible Interaction.** NordiCHI. Defines the CapCode scheme LucentMaps depends on for automatic map identification; without it, LucentMaps is not reproducible.
- **[6] Brock, Truillet, Oriola, Picard, Jouffrais (2015). Interactivity improves usability of geographic maps for visually impaired people.** HCI journal. The reference audio-tactile map study LucentMaps positions itself against. Already in the collection as Brock_2015_InteractiveMapsUsability.
- **[35] Zeng, Miao, Weber (2015). Interactive Audio-haptic Map Explorer on a Tactile Display.** Closest interactive-map competitor on dedicated tactile display hardware. Needed for cost/portability comparison.
- **[36][37] Zeng & Weber (2011, 2012). ATMap and Accessible Maps.** The annotated-tactile-map lineage that predates LucentMaps — shows the state of the art on bespoke hardware that LucentMaps deliberately avoids.
- **[31] Voelker et al. (2013). PUCs.** Foundational work on passive capacitive widgets on unmodified multi-touch displays, which is the technical ancestor of the CapCode approach used here.

## Notes on Reference Extraction Quality

The reference list on p.88-90 of the ASSETS proceedings is dense and a few entries are hard to read in the page image after the white-background flatten because some of the original typesetting uses italics for journal names that render thin in the rasterized PNG. The list above preserves the numbering and authors with high fidelity for refs [29]-[38] (the right-hand column of p.89 and p.90, which are most legible) and is correct-but-summarized for refs [1]-[28] where only the first author and a paraphrased title is reconstructed from the visible text; a re-render at higher DPI would be required to transcribe every entry verbatim. The inline citations throughout notes.md (e.g. "[24]" for Taylor) are the authoritative record of how this paper uses each reference.
