LucentMaps presents a pipeline for producing translucent FDM-3D-printed tactile map overlays in PLA that bond to a consumer smartphone or tablet and embed passive "CapCode" capacitive identification markers so the device auto-identifies which map is sitting on it and can deliver per-feature speech, category filtering, and route guidance via a standard Android app backed by OpenStreetMap.
A small n=5 qualitative study with blind and visually impaired participants near Nuremberg demonstrated feasibility at ~$0.40 of filament and ~1 h print time per ~30×30 cm overlay on a ~$500 consumer printer, with users successfully identifying maps, locating features, and following routes; residual-vision users additionally benefited from the tablet screen shining through the translucent print.
For the inclusive-cartography review, the paper is a canonical 2016 cost/feasibility datum for end-user producible tactile maps and directly informs the review's third research question on tools and technologies for transferable, low-cost accessible cartography.
