## Key Citations for Follow-up

1. **Conway et al. (2020)** — "Audio Description: Making Useful Maps for Blind and Visually Impaired People" — the audio description methodology that produced one of the three passing representations; essential for implementing accessible map descriptions
2. **Aziz et al. (2022)** — "Planning Your Journey in Audio" — auditory route overviews for accessible navigation, directly relevant to the Route knowledge component of the MEP Framework
3. **Hennig et al. (2017)** — "Accessible Web Maps for Visually Impaired Users" — practical recommendations and example solutions for web map accessibility
4. **Froehlich et al. (2025)** — "StreetViewAI" — state-of-the-art AI approach to making street view accessible, represents the next frontier of map accessibility
5. **Siegel and White (1975)** — "The Development of Spatial Representations of Large-Scale Environments" — the foundational spatial cognition theory (landmark → route → survey knowledge) that the entire MEP Framework is built on
