Biggs et al. (2025) propose the Map Equivalent-Purpose (MEP) Framework, a systematic 18-criterion evaluation template for determining whether text-based map representations serve the "equivalent purpose" required by WCAG SC 1.1.1, decomposing map purpose into three items (Generalized, Spatial Information, Spatial Relationships) and 15 equivalency criteria across Landmark, Route, and Survey spatial knowledge areas. Evaluating eight text map representations against visual baselines with 91% intercoder reliability, they find that only three approaches — Audiom interactive maps, MUD maps, and audio descriptions — achieve 100% equivalence, while commonly recommended methods like tables, turn-by-turn directions, and short alt text fail dramatically (averaging 23% equivalency), demonstrating that legacy text map methods are inadequate for WCAG compliance. For this collection, the paper provides the first operationalized definition of "equivalent purpose" for a major category of non-text web content, directly connecting to the web standards and evaluation methods research areas while highlighting that Google Maps (present on 18% of websites) produces alt text that scores 0% on Purpose and 30% on Equivalency.
