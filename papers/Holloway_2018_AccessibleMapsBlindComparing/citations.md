## Key Citations for Follow-up

- **Brock, Truillet, Oriola, Picard, Jouffrais 2015 — Interactivity improves usability of geographic maps for visually impaired people** (ref 9): the closest experimental precedent for interactive tactile-map evaluation; directly comparable methodology.
- **Gual, Puyuelo, Lloveras 2014/2015 — The effect of volumetric (3D) tactile symbols** (refs 21, 28): the prior evidence that 3D symbols aid tactile-key memorisation, which the current paper extends from symbol-recognition to whole-map use.
- **Giraud, Brock, Macé, Jouffrais 2017 — Map learning with a 3D printed interactive small-scale model** (ref 19): independent 3D-printed interactive map study with quantitative space/text memorisation outcomes; natural replication target.
- **Taylor, Dey, Siewiorek, Smailagic 2015 — TactileMaps.net** (ref 48): an existing web-based pipeline for generating 3D-printable tactile maps; relevant to the "automated generation" open problem the paper calls out.
- **Wright, Harris, Sticken 2010 — Best-evidence synthesis on tactile maps and models in O&M** (ref 54): the evidence-synthesis anchor for O&M-training claims; important for any systematic-review scaffolding.
- **Shi, Zelzer, Feng, Azenkot 2016 / Shi, Zhao, Azenkot 2017 — audio-label toolkits for 3D printed models** (refs 30, 31): the closest design-space neighbours to the Interactive Audio Labels prototype; worth direct comparison.
