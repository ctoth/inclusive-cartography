## Key Citations for Follow-up

- **[33] Taylor, Dey, Siewiorek, Smailagic. 2016. Customizable 3D printed tactile maps as interactive overlays.** Direct predecessor for the interactive 3D-printed tactile-map paradigm; likely shares fabrication and interaction ideas with this paper's V2 prototype.
- **[19] Holloway, Marriott, Butler. 2018. Accessible Maps for the Blind: Comparing 3D Printed Models with Tactile Graphics.** Empirical comparison of 3D-printed tactile maps against traditional tactile graphics — useful as a benchmark for 3D vs. 2D tactile formats.
- **[3] Brock, Truillet, Oriola, Picard, Jouffrais. 2015. Interactivity improves usability of geographic maps for visually impaired people.** Establishes the general claim that interactivity (audio + tactile) improves tactile map usability — a foundational result this paper builds on.
- **[35] Wahl. 2013. The psychological challenge of late-life vision impairment.** Motivates the specific focus on older adults who acquired VI late in life — key to framing the paper's underserved audience.
- **[36] Ward & Banks. 2017. Older people's experiences of sight loss in care homes.** Direct context for the residential-care-home use case chosen in this study.
- **[28] Morash, Connell Pensky, Tseng, Miele. 2014. Effects of using multiple hands and fingers on haptic performance in individuals who are blind.** Grounds the ergonomic requirement that the tactile map support two-handed exploration.
