# Abstract

## Original Text (Verbatim)

We present an interactive tactile map tailored for older adults with vision impairments, which should help them to acquire spatial knowledge of complex indoor environments, like a residential care home. Interestingly current research largely omits visually impaired older adults. As the outcome of our iterative design process, we introduced the design of the tactile map that employs haptically salient key objects and large touch-sensitive segments along with the route-guidance function. Route-guidance function enriches the tactile map with the desired route information to support the route knowledge acquisition. Qualitative evaluation (mean age was 81.4 years) indicates the ability of our concept to help our audience building spatial knowledge of the indoor environment.

## Our Interpretation

The paper addresses a neglected sub-population — older adults whose vision impairment has developed later in life and who must learn a novel complex indoor environment such as the residential care home they have moved into. The authors iterate three prototype generations and converge on a 3D-printed composite map with large capacitive touch-sensitive room/corridor segments, haptically salient landmark objects, and an audio layer that supports both free exploration and turn-by-turn route guidance. A qualitative evaluation with 10 VI older adults (mean age 81.4) suggests the design supports survey and route knowledge acquisition and yields design guidelines (scale ~1 cm/m, large distinctive tactile symbols, adjustable speech rate/volume, ergonomic stability) that are directly usable by follow-up work on inclusive indoor cartography.
