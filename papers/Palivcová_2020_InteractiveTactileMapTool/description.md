Palivcová, Macík, and Míkovec present an interactive 3D-printed tactile map tailored to visually impaired older adults living in a residential care home, combining haptically salient landmark objects, large capacitive touch-sensitive room/corridor segments, and an audio route-guidance mode produced through three iterative prototype generations.
A qualitative in-situ evaluation with 10 VI older adults (mean age 81.4, 5F/5M, 62–87) indicates the design helps users acquire both survey and route knowledge of the indoor environment, while surfacing concrete guidelines on map scale (~1 cm/1 m), tactile symbol distinctness, audio rate/volume adjustability, and ergonomic stability.
Directly relevant to inclusive cartography: it is a scarce empirical data point on the intersection of aging and visual impairment, provides a reusable indoor tactile-map design pattern, and illustrates why LLM-confidence thresholds that weigh statistical rigor alone would underrate small-N formative work with high topical value.
