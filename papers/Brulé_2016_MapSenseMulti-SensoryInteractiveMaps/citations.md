## Key Citations for Follow-up

- **[5] Brock, Truillet, Oriola, Picard, Jouffrais (2015) — "Interactivity improves usability of geographic maps for visually impaired people"** — direct prior work by the same authors, the quantitative evaluation counterpart to MapSense. Essential for any inclusive-cartography review that wants effect sizes rather than qualitative themes.
- **[41] Miele, Landau, Gilden — "Talking TMAP"** — defining ancestor system (automated generation of audio-tactile maps from SVG). Comparing Talking TMAP's generative approach with MapSense's D-I-Y authoring is a natural axis for a systematic review.
- **[50] Shams & Seitz — "Benefits of Multisensory Learning"** — cognitive-science grounding for the whole design rationale; any critique of MapSense's qualitative-only evidence should address this theoretical basis.
- **[52] Ullmer & Ishii — "Emerging frameworks for tangible user interfaces"** — foundational TUI framework used by the paper to justify 3D-printed tangibles.
- **[55] Zeng & Weber — "Accessible Maps for the Visually Impaired"** — closely related contemporary system; good for comparative synthesis.
- **[23] Hurst & Tobias — "Empowering Individuals with Do-it-Yourself Assistive Technology"** — grounds the D-I-Y / caretaker-as-author argument.
