## Key Citations for Follow-up

1. **Gual, Puyuelo & Lloveras (2012), Applied Ergonomics 43(6):1032-1040.** The same group's earlier paper analysing volumetric tactile symbols produced by 3D printing — the direct methodological ancestor of this study. Likely contains the symbol-height and material rationale that this paper treats as background.
2. **Gardiner & Perkins (2002), Cartographic Journal 39(2):145-158.** Best-practice guidelines for vacuum-formed tactile maps — the canonical prior-art baseline that the paper measures its volumetric approach against.
3. **Rowell & Ungar (2003a, 2003b), Cartographic Journal 40(3).** International survey of tactile map symbol usage and a proposed symbol taxonomy. Provides the classification framework underlying the paper's 10-symbol set.
4. **Jehoel, McCallum, Rowson & Ungar (2006), British Journal of Visual Impairment 24(2):67-75.** Empirical cognitive-tactualization approach to tactile map design. Directly relevant to how the authors justify training and task structure.
5. **McCallum, Rowell & Ungar (2006), Cartographic Journal 43(3):273-285.** Describes producing tactile maps with inkjet (microcapsule) technology — the production pipeline that the paper's Map A and Map B base layer both rely on.
