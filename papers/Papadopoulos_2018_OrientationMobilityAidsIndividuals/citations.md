## Key Citations for Follow-up

- **Koustriava, Papadopoulos, Koukourikos & Barouti (2016)** — immediate predecessor study from the same group comparing verbal description vs. audio-tactile map on wayfinding; the 2017/2018 paper builds directly on the design introduced there.
- **Papadopoulos et al. (2017), "Comparison of three O&M aids"** — parallel publication extending the comparison to include an audio-haptic map condition; useful for triangulating the present paper's audio-tactile results.
- **Espinosa, Ungar, Ochaita, Blades & Spencer (1998)** — landmark comparison of verbal, tactile and direct-experience methods for learning unfamiliar urban environments; the methodological ancestor of the present study.
- **Ungar, Blades & Spencer (1997)** — strategies for knowledge acquisition from cartographic maps by blind adults; supplies the theoretical framework for why tactile maps encode spatial relations better than serial verbal descriptions.
- **Bradley & Dunlop (2005)** — experimental investigation into wayfinding directions for visually impaired users, verbal vs. other modalities; provides a complementary benchmark on a different population and task.
