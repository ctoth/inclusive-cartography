## Key Citations for Follow-up

- **Tian et al. 2023** - "Just ask for calibration". Closest concurrent work; Top-K is their contribution, and this paper shows Top-K on its own is one instance of a broader framework. Already in collection at `papers/Tian_2023_JustAskCalibrationStrategies/`.
- **Lin et al. 2022** - "Teaching models to express their uncertainty in words". The fine-tuning-based approach that Xiong's black-box framework is positioned against. Already in collection at `papers/Lin_2022_TeachingModelsExpressUncertainty/`.
- **Kadavath et al. 2022** - "Language models (mostly) know what they know". White-box self-evaluation for LLM uncertainty; this paper's closest "big sibling" on the white-box side. Already in collection at `papers/Kadavath_2022_LanguageModelsMostlyKnow/`.
- **Kuhn, Gal, Farquhar 2023** - "Semantic uncertainty". Directly argues that token-likelihood captures the wrong thing (motivation for going black-box). Worth retrieving.
- **Zhou, Jurafsky, Hashimoto 2023** - "Navigating the grey area". Explains why LLMs cluster verbalized confidence in human-like patterns. Worth retrieving.
- **Guo, Pleiss, Sun, Weinberger 2017** - "On calibration of modern neural networks". Foundational ECE paper; cited as the origin of the overconfidence-in-neural-networks literature.
