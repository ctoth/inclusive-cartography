## Key Citations for Follow-up

- **Lin, Hilton, Evans 2022 — "Teaching Models to Express Uncertainty in Words"** (arXiv:2205.14334): the closest complementary work, on verbalized confidences rather than probe-probabilities. Already present in this paper collection.
- **Guo et al. 2017 — "On Calibration of Modern Neural Networks"**: baseline for temperature scaling as a calibration remedy, and the starting point for the RLHF fix in this paper.
- **Srivastava et al. 2022 — BIG-Bench**: the evaluation framework used throughout; needed to understand what "calibrated on BIG-Bench" actually means task-by-task.
- **Cobbe et al. 2021 — GSM8k**: the key OOD benchmark where P(IK) generalization fails most notably; critical for interpreting the confidence-threshold critique.
- **Hendrycks et al. 2020 — MMLU**: the benchmark used for the "None of the Above" stress test, the most dramatic calibration failure in the paper.
