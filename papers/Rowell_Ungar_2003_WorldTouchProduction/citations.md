## Key Citations for Follow-up

- **Espinosa, Ungar, Ochaita, Blades & Spencer (1998)** — empirical comparison of methods for introducing blind people to unfamiliar urban environments; directly connects the Rowell/Ungar production survey to wayfinding outcomes and is one of the strongest empirical backings for the claim that tactile maps actually improve mobility performance.
- **Ungar, Blades, Spencer & Morsley (1994)** — can blind children use tactile maps for direction estimation? A foundational cognitive study from Ungar that grounds the user-side assumptions of the present production survey.
- **Edman, P. (1992) *Tactile Graphics*** — the canonical reference work on tactile graphics design; appears repeatedly in the design-side literature and is the obvious bridge to the companion Part 2 paper.
- **Dacen Nagel & Coulson (1990)** — *Cartographica* comparative study of tactual mobility maps; directly relevant to cartographic-design claims in the inclusive-cartography seed corpus.
- **Vasconcellos (1993, 1994)** — two conference papers on tactile-map design for visually impaired users from an ICC / NACIS context; flagged as under-cited in the design literature, and worth pulling for the seed corpus as a non-Anglophone production perspective.
