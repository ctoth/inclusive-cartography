## Key Citations for Follow-up

- **[15]** Burstall & Goguen, "Putting theories together to make specifications" (1977) — direct predecessor that handles only equational logic; the whole paper is a generalization of this.
- **[77]** Meseguer, "General logics" (1989) — the closest contemporary rival formulation, based on closure-system consequence rather than model-theoretic satisfaction. Worth reading for comparison.
- **[6]** Barwise, "Axioms for abstract model theory" (1974) — classical model-theoretic abstract-logic predecessor.
- **[97]–[100]** Tarlecki's "Bits and pieces" and follow-ups — subsequent development of institution-independent model theory and cocompleteness results.
- **[46]** Goguen & Burstall, "A study in the foundations of programming methodology: Specifications, institutions, charters and parchments" (1986) — the authors' own earlier systematic-foundation paper introducing adjoint charter/parchment structures, which this JACM paper refines.
