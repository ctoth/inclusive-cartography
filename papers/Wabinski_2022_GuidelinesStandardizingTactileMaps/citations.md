## Key Citations for Follow-up

- **Nolan & Morris (1971)** — *Improvement of Tactual Symbols for Blind Children: Final Report*. The canonical empirical source for Wabinski's recommended point/line/area symbol inventory. If the project runs its own symbol-discriminability study, this is the primary comparator.
- **Wiedel & Groves (1969)** — early vacuum-formed empirical distinguishability study that anchors several of the Appendix A rows; still cited as the "most discrete tactually" baseline.
- **BANA/CBA (2010)** — *Guidelines and Standards for Tactile Graphics*. The most-cited practitioner guideline in the review; competing reference point against NSW 2006 and ISO 19028.
- **ISO 19028 (2016)** — international standard for tactile guide maps; candidate anchor for any attempt at a "global standard" and a source the 2026 review protocol should weigh against practitioner documents.
- **Perkins (2002)** — "Cartography: Progress in Tactile Mapping" — the most direct prior review paper to this one; useful as a benchmark for what changed in 20 years of tactile-map research.
- **Topfer & Pillewizer (1966)** — the sqrt(M_o/M_f) generalization rule applied in Wabinski's discussion; load-bearing for any automated tactile-map generalization step in the project.
- **Holloway, Marriott, Reinders & Butler (2019)** — empirical comparison of 3D-printed maps vs traditional tactile graphics "in the wild"; directly relevant to the production-method sensitivity question that Wabinski flags as open.
- **Giudice, Guenther, Jensen & Haase (2020)** — multimodal (touchscreen vs embossed) wayfinding study; relevant to Wabinski's acknowledged multimodal gap.
- **Mukhiddinov & Kim (2021)** — systematic review of *automatic* tactile-graphics creation; the closest prior art for a tactile-map automation pipeline.
