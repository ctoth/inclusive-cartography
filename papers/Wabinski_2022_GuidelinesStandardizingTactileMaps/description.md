Wabinski, Moscicka and Touya review decades of empirical research and practitioner best-practice documents to consolidate measurable design guidelines for tactile maps — covering symbol inventories, minimum dimensions/spacings/heights, generalization rules, and how production method (thermoforming, swell-paper, braille embosser, 3D printing) constrains symbol choice.
They extract concrete parameter tables (Tables 2-4 on p.12) and a category-organized appendix of recommended, empirically distinguishable symbols (Appendix A, pp.18-20), and explicitly argue that no global tactile-map design standard yet exists despite a mature evidence base from Nolan & Morris, Wiedel & Groves, BANA/CBA, NSW, and ISO.
This paper is the inclusive-cartography project's single best reference for the numeric invariants any automated tactile-map pipeline must respect and directly motivates the 2026 systematic-review protocol authored by the same lead author.
