## Key Citations for Follow-up

1. **Edman, P.K. (1992) *Tactile Graphics*** — The standard reference book on tactile graphic design and production. Cited repeatedly throughout the paper as the source of the production-method taxonomy.
2. **Perkins, C. (2002) "Cartography: progress in tactile mapping"** — Progress-in-field companion review article, likely the most useful secondary source for situating Rowell & Ungar's survey results in a broader research landscape.
3. **James, G.A. and Armstrong, J.D. (1975) "Handbook for mobility maps"** — Foundational British handbook for tactile mobility mapping; represents the pre-standardisation state that this survey is measuring against.
4. **Aldrich, Sheppard & Hindle (2002) "First steps towards a model of tactile graphicacy"** — Contemporary theoretical framing that complements Rowell & Ungar's empirical picture.
5. **Bentzen (1997) "Orientation aids" in *Foundations of Orientation and Mobility*** — User-side perspective on how tactile maps are integrated into orientation and mobility training for visually impaired travellers.
