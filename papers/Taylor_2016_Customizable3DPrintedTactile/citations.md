## Key Citations for Follow-up

- **[21] Landau & Gourgey 2001 — Development of a Talking Tactile Tablet.** The canonical Touch Graphics TTT reference that Taylor et al. explicitly position themselves against; any protocol work claiming "interactive tactile map" priors should read this.
- **[22] Miele, Landau & Gilden 2006 — Talking TMAP.** The automated audio-tactile map generator the paper cites as the closest interactive-map production system; directly relevant to our authoring-pipeline comparison.
- **[12] Götzelmann 2016 — LucentMaps.** Same ASSETS 2016 venue, same 3D-printed-tactile-map direction, uses a different (visual + tactile) overlay strategy. Pair-reading LucentMaps with this paper is the cleanest way to see the design-space for 3D-printed inclusive maps in 2016.
- **[13] Götzelmann 2014 — Automatically generated tactile detail maps by 3D printers.** Upstream prior art for automatic OSM → 3D print; crucial to cite when establishing that Taylor et al.'s contribution is the *interactive* overlay, not the print pipeline itself.
- **[7][8] Brock et al. — Touch the map! / Design and User Satisfaction of Interactive Maps.** Core references for interactive tactile-map usability with blind users; useful priors for any user-study design in our downstream work.
- **[29] Watanabe et al. 2014 — Tactile map automated creation system using OpenStreetMap.** Most direct OSM-based prior to Taylor et al.'s authoring pipeline.
