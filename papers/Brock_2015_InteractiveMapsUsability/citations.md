## Key Citations for Follow-up

- **Parkes (1988) — NOMAD**: The original audio-tactile interactive map system. Essential historical context for any work on interactive tactile maps for the visually impaired.
- **Miele, Landau, & Gilden (2006) — Talking TMAP**: Earlier audio-tactile map system using double-tap interactions; directly contrasted with this paper's single-touch design choice.
- **Brooke (1996) — SUS**: The validated usability instrument used here; relevant anywhere satisfaction is measured.
- **Siegel & White (1975) — Landmark/Route/Survey taxonomy**: Spatial cognition framework used to structure the 18-question test (L/R/S).
- **Thinus-Blanc & Gaunet (1997) — Representation of space in blind persons**: Foundational review of blind spatial cognition — relevant to evaluating whether tactile maps produce survey-type mental representations.
- **Hegarty et al. (2002) — Santa Barbara Sense of Direction Scale**: Used as a participant-characteristic covariate.
- **McGookin, Brewster & Jiang (2008)**: Key prior work on touchscreen accessibility limitations that motivated the single-touch design.
- **Kane, Bigham & Wobbrock — SlideRule**: Related touchscreen accessibility design.
