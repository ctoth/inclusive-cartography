## Key Citations for Follow-up

- **Kadavath et al. 2022, "Language Models (Mostly) Know What They Know"** — the P(True)/P(IK) baseline that Tian et al. beat. Essential background for any critique of RLHF-LM calibration claims.
- **Lin, Hilton & Evans 2022a, "Teaching Models to Express Their Uncertainty in Words"** — prior work on verbalized uncertainty; already present in this project's collection as `Lin_2022_TeachingModelsExpressUncertainty`.
- **Guo et al. 2017, "On Calibration of Modern Neural Networks"** — the reference for temperature scaling; defines the canonical ECE formulation used throughout.
- **Xiao et al. 2022, "Uncertainty Quantification with Pre-trained Language Models"** — another large-scale empirical study of LM calibration; useful to triangulate Tian et al.'s claims.
- **Kuhn, Gal & Farquhar 2023, "Semantic Uncertainty"** — alternative uncertainty-estimation method for generative LMs that operates on meaning rather than tokens; complementary to "just ask".
- **Zhou, Jurafsky & Hashimoto 2023, "Navigating the grey area"** — characterizes how LMs express overconfidence vs. uncertainty; useful for interpreting the linguistic-expression histograms in Tian et al.'s appendix.
